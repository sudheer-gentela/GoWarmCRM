What GoWarmCRM Fixes

Real problems. Specific fixes. Not features.

Most CRM platforms sell you capabilities. GoWarmCRM is built around the exact failures that cost B2B sales teams the most revenue — and the mechanisms to fix them.

Problem 01 — Pipeline Health

Deals go dark between stages and nobody notices

Your pipeline report shows 40 open deals. But 12 of them haven't had a meaningful activity touch in over 3 weeks. Nobody can see it — because the CRM shows "In Progress" until someone manually moves it.

  • Nightly diagnostic rules engine scores every deal by age, stage duration, and rep activity
  • Alerts fire automatically when a deal crosses your configured risk threshold
  • Snooze settings and alert age tracking persist — no daily reset, no loss of context
  • Managers see a live risk dashboard, not a weekly report that's already stale
See it in a demo
How GoWarmCRM Fixes It
The Deals Diagnostic Engine

Every night, a rules engine evaluates your entire deals pipeline. It checks days since last activity, stage duration against your configured benchmarks, and whether the assigned rep has any upcoming tasks.

When a deal breaches a threshold, an action alert is created — or updated if one already exists. The alert carries age metadata so managers can see how long it's been flagged.

28%
Of sales reps with CRM access don't use it consistently — meaning pipeline data is incomplete by design. (Cirrus Insight, 2025)
Problem 02 — Playbook Adoption

Playbooks that exist on paper but nowhere in the workflow

You invested in a sales playbook. Your enablement team built it out. Reps were trained on it. Six months later, win rates haven't moved — because nobody opens the document at the moment they actually need it.

  • Playbook plays are wired directly into the action engine as task queues
  • Stage changes automatically trigger the next required play for the rep
  • Completion chains ensure the next play only fires when the prior one is done
  • Managers see play completion rates by rep, stage, and deal type
See it in a demo
How GoWarmCRM Fixes It
Event-Triggered Play Execution

When a deal moves to a new stage, GoWarmCRM fires the first play in your configured playbook for that stage. The rep sees it as an action item — not a document reference.

Plays chain automatically. Complete play 1, play 2 appears. Skip it, and it shows up in the manager's diagnostic view as an incomplete step on an active deal.

~60%
Of sales teams without a structured, enforced process miss quota — regardless of rep quality. (Harvard Business Review, via The Sales Collective)
Problem 03 — Forecast Accuracy

A forecast built on rep optimism instead of activity data

Every quarter, the forecast is manually assembled from rep updates, CRM stage values, and gut feel. It's wrong by 20–35% every time. The problem isn't the people — it's a process that has no structural data to work from.

  • ARR timing rules enforce structured recognition criteria — no more creative stage labelling
  • Activity signals (call logs, email threads, task completion) feed forecast confidence scores
  • Diagnostic alerts flag deals where stage and activity are out of sync
  • Forecast views separate structured data from rep commentary
See it in a demo
How GoWarmCRM Fixes It
Activity-Signal Forecasting

GoWarmCRM builds forecast confidence from deal activity data — not stage labels. A deal at stage 4 with no activity in 14 days is flagged differently than one with 3 logged calls this week.

ARR recognition rules ensure that what's in the forecast actually meets your close criteria. No more deals sitting at "Verbal Commit" for 6 weeks.

Only 20%
Of sales organisations achieve forecasts within 5% of actual results. 43% miss by 10% or more. (Xactly Sales Forecasting Benchmark Report, 2024)
Problem 04 — Handover Quality

Sales-to-CS handover commitments that disappear after close

The AE promises onboarding support, a custom integration, and a 60-day check-in. The deal closes. The CS team gets a Slack message and a Salesforce record. Two of those commitments never happen. The customer churns at month 8.

  • Handover commitments are logged as structured objects at deal close — not in notes fields
  • Each commitment has an owner, a due date, and a completion state
  • Nightly sweep alerts fire when commitments are overdue or at risk
  • CS team sees a live handover dashboard, not a backlog of Slack messages
See it in a demo
How GoWarmCRM Fixes It
Structured Handover Tracking

At deal close, the AE creates a handover record with specific commitments — each one a tracked object with an owner and deadline. Not a note. Not a checklist in a doc.

The handover rules engine checks completion status nightly and surfaces alerts when commitments are approaching or past their due dates.

47%
Of CRM users say their platform had a significant impact on customer retention — but only when post-sale commitments are actually tracked, not buried in notes. (Capterra)
Problem 05 — Contract Lifecycle

Contracts that auto-renew into silence — or into churn

Your CLM tool tracks contract status. But when a contract moves to "Under Review" or "Pending Renewal," nothing happens automatically. The CS team finds out when the customer calls to cancel.

  • Every contract stage change fires a trigger into the action engine
  • Configurable rules determine which stage transitions require action, and what kind
  • Renewal risk scoring runs nightly on all active contracts
  • No manual CLM review required — the system tells you what needs attention
See it in a demo
How GoWarmCRM Fixes It
CLM Event Triggers

GoWarmCRM hooks into your contract lifecycle at every stage change. When a contract moves, a configured action is generated — or a diagnostic alert is raised if the move signals risk.

No polling. No manual review queues. Every contract stage change is an event that produces a response in your team's action queue.

30–70%
Of CRM deployments fail — most commonly because processes aren't enforced at the workflow level. Contract lifecycle is one of the most commonly untracked areas. (C5 Insight)
Problem 06 — Prospecting Clarity

Prospects in your pipeline with no clear next move

SDRs have 200 prospects in various states of engagement. Some are hot. Most aren't. The problem is that nobody can tell which is which from the CRM — so everyone gets the same generic cadence and half the pipeline is wasted effort.

  • Every prospect gets a live hurdle score based on your configured criteria
  • The prospecting engine identifies exactly what's blocking each deal
  • SDRs see a prioritised action queue — not an undifferentiated prospect list
  • Nightly sweep re-scores all prospects so stale data doesn't mislead effort
See it in a demo
How GoWarmCRM Fixes It
ProspectDiagnosticsEngine

Each night, the prospecting engine evaluates every active prospect against your configured hurdle criteria — engagement level, ICP fit, activity recency, stage duration.

Prospects that score below threshold get a diagnostic action generated with a specific recommended next step. The SDR sees what to do — not just who to call.

21%
Average B2B sales win rate. With 4 in 5 opportunities lost, who you prioritise — and when — is the difference between pipeline and noise. (HubSpot, 2023)

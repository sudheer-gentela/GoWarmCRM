The problem with playbooks today

You wrote the playbook. Six months later, no one's running it.

Every sales org has a playbook. Stage-gates, qualification frameworks, follow-up cadences, handover checklists. The playbook itself is rarely the problem — adherence is. Reps don't open documents at the moment they need them. Managers can't audit what didn't happen. The playbook becomes a training artifact, not an operating system.

GoWarm Skills closes that gap by making each play executable. The skill watches for its trigger condition (a stage transition, a stalled deal, a meeting just ended), reads the live deal context, and drafts the next action the play prescribes. The rep approves, edits, or skips. The play actually runs.

How a skill works

A folder. Markdown. YAML. That's it.

A GoWarm Skill is a directory with a single SKILL.md file plus optional templates and reference data. The format follows the open Agent Skills standard, originally developed by Anthropic. Portable, version-controlled, readable by humans and agents.

skills/discovery-call-prep/SKILL.md --- name: discovery-call-prep description: Generates pre-call preparation: a brief, a talk track, two follow-up email variants, and a recommended next play. Use whenever a deal enters the discovery_call stage or a meeting is scheduled within 24 hours. --- # Discovery Call Prep You are executing the Discovery Call Prep play from the GoWarm Sales module. Turn the deal payload into four deliverables the rep can act on immediately. ## Required inputs - prospect, account, deal, interaction_history, meddpicc ## Output - prep_brief - talk_track (with discovery questions) - follow_up_email_strong - follow_up_email_weak - recommended_next_play

When the trigger condition fires, GoWarm runs the skill against the live deal context and surfaces the output in the rep's action queue. The rep sees the brief, scans the talk track, and picks one of the email drafts — all before opening their inbox.

What makes this different

Generic AI drafts emails. Skills run plays.

There are dozens of "AI for sales" tools. Almost all of them draft email or summarize a call. None of them know your playbook. GoWarm Skills are different because the playbook itself is the input.

Shipping with

A starter library mapped to the GoWarm playbook.

Early access ships with a curated set of skills covering the highest-leverage moments in a B2B sales cycle. Each skill is a real, runnable play from the GoWarm canonical playbook — and a starting point for you to fork and customize.

discovery-call-prep
Discovery Call Prep
Generates a pre-call brief, talk track with discovery questions, two follow-up email variants (strong and weak signal), and a recommended next play.
stall-recovery
Stall Recovery
Triggered when a deal goes 14+ days without two-way contact. Drafts a pattern-interrupt email tuned to the deal's last known objection or stage.
handover-confirmation
Handover Confirmation
Fires at deal close. Captures the AE's verbal commitments and creates tracked tasks for the CSM with delivery deadlines.
renewal-outreach
Renewal Outreach
Fires 90 days before contract end. Reads usage and engagement signals, drafts a renewal-positioning email tuned to the account's health score.
qualification-deep-dive
Qualification Deep-Dive
Triggered at SAL stage. Reviews MEDDPICC completeness and drafts the specific discovery questions needed to close the qualification gaps.
multi-thread-prompt
Multi-Thread Prompt
Fires when a deal over $50K has only one engaged contact for 21+ days. Drafts an outreach to a second stakeholder, identified from interaction patterns.

Plus a skill authoring UI for forking, customizing, and version-controlling your own. Your playbook is yours — your skills should be too.

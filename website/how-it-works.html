How GoWarmCRM Works

Your pipeline is diagnosed every night.
Your team wakes up knowing exactly what to do.

GoWarmCRM is the execution layer that sits between your CRM and your revenue team — standardising how every rep prioritises, how every playbook fires, and how every manager sees risk. It's not a productivity tool. It's the operating system your revenue org runs on.

Built for 5–200 rep B2B sales teams Works alongside your existing CRM Live within 1–2 weeks, no rep retraining AI on every action, human approval always

From raw CRM data to daily action — automatically

Three phases run every night, for every entity in your pipeline. By the time your team opens their laptops, the work of triaging the pipeline is already done.

Phase 01
Diagnose every deal, contract, and prospect

Rules engines run nightly across your entire pipeline — reading signals from emails, calendar events, and meeting patterns, not just from CRM fields. Every deal is scored by stage duration, last two-way email contact, and calendar activity. Every contract is checked against renewal timelines. Every prospect is scored against your configured hurdle criteria. Anything that breaches a threshold gets flagged automatically, whether or not a rep updated a field.

Deal stall detection

Scored by days in stage, last two-way email contact, and calendar activity — read directly from email and calendar data, not from logged CRM tasks. Thresholds configurable per stage and deal value bracket.

Contract risk monitoring

CLM stage changes trigger action queues automatically. Renewal windows, ARR timing rules, and at-risk contracts all surfaced before you'd normally notice them.

Prospect hurdle scoring

Every prospect scored nightly against your criteria. GoWarmCRM surfaces exactly what's blocking progression — and what action would unblock it.

Handover commitment tracking

Every commitment logged at deal close. Nightly sweep monitors delivery status. Alerts fire when a commitment is approaching or past its due date.

Where GoWarmCRM reads signals from

Email threads (last two-way contact, response latency) · Calendar events (meetings scheduled, frequency) · Meeting patterns (cadence, drop-off) · CRM fields (stage, value, owner) — four sources, not one. So your diagnostics work even when CRM data is incomplete.

What this replaces
The Monday morning pipeline review where a manager asks "what's the latest on Acme?" — and the answer is whatever the rep remembered to log.
Phase 02
Build a prioritised action queue for every rep

Diagnostic alerts don't sit in a log. They become a live, ranked action queue — ordered by urgency, deal value, and time-at-risk. Unlike static task lists that go stale the moment they're generated, GoWarmCRM actions persist intelligently: snooze a deal, and the alert comes back harder if conditions worsen. Complete a playbook step, and the next one fires automatically.

Upsert-and-resolve logic

Actions updated nightly with conflict-safe logic. Existing actions are refreshed, never duplicated. Resolved when the underlying condition clears.

Playbook triggers on stage change

Deal moves to a new stage → the configured playbook fires. Rep sees the required next step in their queue, not in a document they won't open.

Priority ranking

Actions ranked by urgency, deal value, and days-at-risk. The most important thing is always at the top. Every morning, without exception.

Snooze and supersede

Reps snooze with context. If conditions worsen during the snooze window, GoWarmCRM supersedes the snoozed action automatically — no silent slippage.

What this replaces
The rep deciding each morning which deals to focus on — a decision shaped more by recency bias and inbox noise than pipeline reality.
Phase 03
AI enforces execution — one action at a time

For every alert in the queue, GoWarmCRM's AI drafts a specific recommended next action. Not a generic nudge — a concrete suggestion written in context: the deal history, the reason for the alert, the stage the deal is at, and the rep's own communication style. The rep sees the suggestion, approves or edits it, and it goes. The manager sees what was done.

Context-aware suggestions

Every AI suggestion is written against the specific alert — deal value, stall duration, last activity, and stage context all inform the output. Not a template. A tailored action.

Human-in-the-loop always

Nothing sends without approval. Reps review, edit, or reject every suggestion. AI accelerates the decision — it doesn't make it.

Per-user configuration

Every rep configures their own AI preferences — provider, verbosity, tone — with org-level defaults that cascade. An AE and an SDR get AI tuned to their role, not a shared generic prompt.

Module-level prompt tuning

AI behaviour configurable per pipeline area. Deal suggestions, contract risk flags, and prospecting briefs can each have different tone, depth, and format.

What this replaces
The rep staring at a stalled deal for two minutes, deciding whether it's worth sending a "just checking in" email that won't move the needle.

AI Configuration

AI that enforces execution. Not a black box.

GoWarmCRM's AI isn't a chatbot you interrogate. It's an execution enforcer embedded in the action queue — writing the specific next action for every alert, for every rep, in the context of their actual deal situation.

Every suggestion is visible, editable, and requires approval. Nothing goes to a customer without a human seeing it first. The AI accelerates good judgment — it doesn't bypass it.

Because different roles need different intelligence, every user configures their own preferences. An AE's deal suggestions are written differently to an SDR's prospecting briefs — same engine, role-appropriate output.

  • → Per-user AI provider selection (OpenAI, Anthropic, others)
  • → Module-level prompt tuning — deals, contracts, prospecting
  • → Org-level defaults with user-level overrides
  • → Human-in-the-loop approval before anything is actioned
  • → Tone and verbosity controls per user and per module

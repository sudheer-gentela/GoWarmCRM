# Industry Benchmarks: Sales Execution Gaps

> Source citations available at https://www.gowarmcrm.com/blog (full article series on pipeline execution).

## Stall rate benchmarks

The proportion of active pipeline that goes "dark" (no meaningful activity for 14+ days) without any structured next-action prompt:

- **Low-end**: 20% — well-managed teams with disciplined CRM hygiene
- **Central**: 25% — typical mid-market B2B SaaS team
- **High-end**: 30% — teams with heavy outbound or weak playbook adherence

Source basis: HubSpot State of Sales reports, Bridge Group SaaS benchmarks, RevOps community surveys.

## Recovery rate benchmarks

The proportion of stalled pipeline that can be recovered when reps receive structured next-action prompts at the moment of stall:

- **Low-end**: 35% — when prompts are surfaced but rep approval rate is low
- **Central**: 40% — typical when AI-drafted actions reach 60%+ approval rate
- **High-end**: 45% — high-discipline teams with strong CSM/AE handoff loops

Source basis: Internal GoWarmCRM customer outcome data and published execution-layer studies.

## Why these numbers

The "44% follow up only once / 80% of deals need 5+ touches" gap (HubSpot, Bridge Group) is the underlying mechanic. Most pipeline rot is a persistence failure, not a fit failure — so structured prompts at the right moment recover meaningful revenue.

## Confidence framing

- For pipelines under $1M: estimate may overstate recovery; small teams have less leverage from a structured prompt system.
- For pipelines $1M–$50M: central estimates are reliable.
- For pipelines over $50M: enterprise dynamics (procurement cycles, multi-stakeholder buying) reduce the marginal recovery rate; use low-end recovery (35%).
